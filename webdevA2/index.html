History of Pears

Pears were a valuable and much-desired commodity among the trading routes of the ancient world. Evident in the works of Renaissance Masters, pears have long been an elegant still-life muse for artists. In the 17th century a great flourishing of modern pear variety cultivation began taking place in Europe. And in popular culture, the pear tree was immortalized alongside a partridge in the 18th-century Christmas carol, The Twelve Days of Christmas.

Types of Pears

With over 3000 types of pear varieties being officially documented, I obviously cannot put them all here, so I have selected some of my favourite ones to be put here.

Asian Pear

Asian pears are large and juicy and they have a higher water content than most European pear varieties. This water content gives the fruit a delicious texture when eaten raw, but makes them less suitable for baking and jam making.

Comice Pear

Comice pears are often considered the sweetest variety of pear. The pulp of these pears is very juicy and sweet, but the skin is delicate and bruises easily presenting challenges to commercial growers.

Bartlett Pear

The Bartlett has the classic pear shape: a round bell on the bottom half, then a distinct shoulder with a smaller neck. Bartletts are highly aromatic pears and have a buttery mouthfeel. Besides eating them fresh, you can enjoy their delicious flavour and smooth texture in a wide variety of dishes.

Concorde Pear

The Concorde is characterised by its long neck, almost pointed top, and long and curved stem.The Concorde is sweet and juicy with dense flesh.You can eat it fresh, slice it into salads, or cook it in various ways, such as roasting, grilling and poaching.

Williams Pear

They are medium to large and they have thin skin that transforms from green to golden yellow as it ripens. The flesh is fine-grained smooth and juicy in texture, while the flavour is aromatic, tart, buttery and sweet when fully ripe.

Red Anjou Pear

When ripe, these pears develop a mildly sweet flavour. They are extremely juicy with a smooth texture, making them a delightful fruity snack.

Fun Facts about Pears

Fact 1

There are over 3000 varieties of pears worldwide.

Fact 2

The United States is one of the largest producers of pears in the world.

Fact 3

A medium pear has about 100 calories.

Fact 4

Although pears mature on the tree, they ripen best off the tree.

Fact 5

Pears are one of the few fruits that have an extensive history, reaching back to about 1000 B.C.

Fact 6

A virtual singer, Kasane Teto, has her face on a pear and produced music with it.

Fact 7

Pears are a part of the rose family.

Fact 8

Pear tree wood is oftentimes used to construct musical instruments, furniture, and other wooden décor.
